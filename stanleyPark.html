Project Brief
-

November 2017

An informational app on Stanley Park, designed for locals and tourists who want to know the meanings behind particular landmarks or art pieces from a First Nations perspective.

The current City of Vancouver Website (as of Fall 2017) only explains what each landmark and art piece is, but does not explain why it is important to First Nations People.

Context
-

Over the semester (4 months), we explored the basic functions of Android Studio such as intents, sensor access, saving user information, and implementation of Google Maps. As this was an individual project, I was able to create my app in a month.

Role
-

Most of my planning was done through site maps and listing out the concepts in order to carry out the functionality to link to other events. I planned out my UI through Illustrator to help visualize the basic user experience of how people are taken through the app.

Take-aways
-

I was able to learn basic Android functionality of an app and how to interact with different screens and other apps. The theoretical part of Android is quite simple, but the actual implementation is quite difficult using Android Studio (as with most code).
